Small FAQ

Why did you make this?

Mostly as a proof-of-concept. When I went to public school during the late 2000's and 2010's, YouTube would always be inaccessible whether it be a Network/Router Block or the device itself was prohibited to load videos (i.e. Chromebooks). While VPNs worked fine, I always seeked for a better alternative. I'm no longer in public school but the idea of a YouTube clone always floated in my head.

How does this work?

LeahTube bypasses network restrictions by simply taking advantage of Public Google APIs. No company or school would block Google.com, so LeahTube fetches all data through Google (or alternative web scrapers) and loads the html plaintext into the browser client, which we can process.

I see jQuery in the sources, this isn't vanilla!

I only use jQuery on here for easily writing data to the page. All web scraping and video processing are done entirely in Vanilla.

Who's Leah?

A friend who I decided to name this after, and the designer of the LeahTube logo.

What future features will be added?

First and foremost will be Channels. Playlists might join as well, and if there's any way of fetching subscriptions I won't have a problem with that either.
